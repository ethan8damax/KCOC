Who We Are

The Kennesaw church of Christ is a simple, Bible-based congregation with one goal: to follow the faith and practice of the church described in the New Testament. We are not a denomination. We are a local congregation of Christians striving to follow the pattern of the early church as revealed in Scripture.

We believe the Bible is the inspired, infallible, and all-sufficient Word of God (2 Timothy 3:16–17; 2 Peter 1:20–21). The New Testament is our final authority for faith, doctrine, worship, and church practice. We don't follow human creeds or traditions — only the Scriptures.

Each local congregation of the church of Christ is autonomous, governed only by Christ through His Word rather than by denominational councils or conventions (Ephesians 1:22–23).

We are a young congregation, and we would love for you to be part of what God is building here. Our desire is to practice first-century Christianity — honoring Christ as the head of His church and encouraging one another to remain faithful to Him.

The Plan of Salvation

One of the most important things we can share is what the Bible teaches about salvation. We believe salvation is offered by the grace of God through the gospel of Jesus Christ, and that the New Testament teaches a clear path of faith and obedience:

Hear the Gospel — Faith begins with hearing the message of Christ (Romans 10:17).

Believe in Jesus Christ — Belief in Him as the Son of God is essential (John 8:24; Mark 16:16).

Repent of Sins — God calls all people to turn away from sin and live according to His will (Acts 17:30).

Confess Christ — Believers openly confess their faith in Jesus as Lord (Romans 10:9–10).

Be Baptized for the Remission of Sins — The New Testament teaches baptism as the moment sins are forgiven and a person is united with Christ (Acts 2:38; Acts 22:16; Romans 6:3–4).

Live Faithfully as a Christian — Christians are called to remain faithful to Christ throughout their lives (Revelation 2:10).

When a person obeys the gospel, God adds them to His church (Acts 2:47).

The Bible as Our Authority

Everything we teach and practice comes from the Scriptures. Our beliefs are shaped by careful study of the New Testament — not by religious tradition or human organizations. The Bible guides us in understanding the nature of God and salvation, how the church is organized and led, how the New Testament church worshipped, the mission and work of the church, and how Christians are called to live.

Christ alone governs His church through His Word, and we strive to follow His instructions faithfully.

Worship

Our worship is simple, reverent, and centered on God and His Word. We follow the example and instruction of the New Testament:

Singing: We sing a cappella — without instrumental accompaniment — encouraging one another through psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs (Ephesians 5:19; Colossians 3:16).

Prayer: Prayers are offered to God by faithful Christian men on behalf of the congregation.

Preaching and Teaching: The preaching of God's Word is central to every assembly.

The Lord's Supper: We observe the Lord's Supper every first day of the week in remembrance of the sacrifice of Christ (Acts 20:7).

Giving: Members give freewill offerings on the first day of the week to support the work of the church (1 Corinthians 16:1–2; 2 Corinthians 9:7). Our work is funded through these voluntary contributions, not fundraising activities.

Leadership

The New Testament teaches that local congregations are led by qualified elders who shepherd the church (1 Timothy 3; Titus 1). Deacons serve as qualified servants assisting in the congregation's work, while evangelists devote themselves to preaching and teaching the Word.

We are a young congregation currently led by spiritually mature men as we grow toward appointing elders and deacons according to the New Testament pattern.

The Work of the Church

The New Testament describes three primary areas of work for the church:

Evangelism: Sharing the gospel of Jesus Christ with others.

Edification: Encouraging and strengthening Christians in their faith.

Benevolence: Using the church treasury to assist fellow Christians who are in need.

Christian Living

Christians are called to lives of holiness, moral integrity, and obedience to Christ. The Bible teaches that marriage is a covenant between one man and one woman, and Christians are called to pursue purity, faithfulness, and godliness in their relationships and daily conduct.

Our Commitment

The Kennesaw church of Christ is committed to:

Following the Bible as our only authority

Restoring the faith and practice of the first-century church

Honoring Christ as the head of His church

Encouraging one another to live faithfully

Helping as many people as possible come to know Christ and reach heaven

We would love to have you worship with us. You are always welcome here.
